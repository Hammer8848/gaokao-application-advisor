# Career Market Analysis For Gaokao Majors

Use this reference when a Gaokao recommendation needs employment, salary, career-development, or industry-development analysis.

## Source Priority

Prefer current sources, with publication dates:

1. University graduate employment quality reports / 毕业生就业质量报告
2. Major-level employment reports from schools or colleges
3. Ministry of Education, Ministry of Human Resources and Social Security, and National Bureau of Statistics data
4. Industry association reports and official industry policy plans
5. Reputable recruitment-platform reports from the latest 1-3 years
6. Employer demand signals from campus recruitment, public job postings, and professional qualification requirements

Use recruitment-platform salary data as directional, not definitive. It is biased toward platform users, cities, industries, and job types.

## Five-Year Market Lens

For each recommended major, assess:

- Entry-level salary band: realistic range, not just top cases
- 3-5 year salary and role progression
- Demand trend: rising, stable, cyclical, saturated, or declining
- Geographic concentration: first-tier cities, provincial capitals, industrial clusters, hospitals, schools, government, or state-owned enterprises
- Education dependency: undergraduate enough, master's preferred, doctorate needed,规培 required,资格证 required, or考公/考编 common
- Industry volatility: policy exposure, real-estate cycle, internet hiring cycle, semiconductor cycle, energy transition, healthcare reform, education policy, or AI automation
- Transferability: whether skills transfer across industries and cities

## Common Interpretation Rules

Computer science, software engineering, AI, data science, electronic information, microelectronics, automation, and cyber security often have higher salary ceilings, but outcomes vary sharply by school platform, city, internships, projects, math/programming ability, and market cycles.

Clinical medicine, stomatology, pharmacy, veterinary medicine, and normal-university public-funded teacher tracks can be stable but may require long training, licensing,规培,考编, or region-specific service obligations.

Electrical engineering, energy, transportation, mechanical engineering, materials, chemical engineering, safety engineering, water conservancy, surveying, GIS, geology, and geophysics can have strong state-owned-enterprise or industrial demand, but location, worksite conditions, and industry cycle matter.

Finance, economics, accounting, law, journalism, language, management, and business majors are highly platform- and city-dependent. They can be good at strong schools or with clear professional credentials, but should not be treated as universally high-paying.

Biology, environment, agriculture, food science, public health, psychology, and basic sciences often need graduate study or a clear professional route for stronger outcomes. Recommend them when the student accepts this path or the school has unusually strong discipline resources.

Teacher education, public administration, police-related, military, navigation, aviation, and special-plan majors must be checked for service obligations, physical requirements, political review, gender/height/vision limits, and employment destination constraints.

## Output Requirements

When recommending a school, always name the recommended major(s) or院校专业组. Do not list a school alone.

For each school-major recommendation, include:

- Admission band: 冲 / 稳 / 保 / 兜底
- School and city
- Recommended major(s)
- Why this school-major combination is worth filling
- Employment/salary outlook
- Industry trend and 3-5 year path
- Main risk or tradeoff
- Verification needed before final submission

If reliable current salary data is unavailable, say so and provide qualitative analysis instead of inventing numbers.
